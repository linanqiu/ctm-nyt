japan 's index of leading indicators rose to 63.6 in august , above the so-called boom-or-bust line of 50 for the first time since may , the economic planning agency said . the leading index recovered from july 's revised level of 36.6 on strong performances in consumer durables and machinery orders , among other factors , according to an agency spokeswoman . the index is intended to measure future economic performance . a figure above 50 indicates 0 the economy is likely to expand ; one below 50 indicates 0 a contraction may be ahead .
